The World of Aethelgard

Ten continents. Each one a civilisation, a climate, a way of war.
From the frozen peaks of Frostpeak to the corrupted darkness of Shadowvale.
Every realm is a different game.

The heartland of civilisation and unity. Temperate, fertile, and endlessly contested.

The Northern Dominion

The largest continent. Ice, endurance, and oaths sworn in the cold that never breaks.

The Story of Aethelgard

Before the first castle rose, there was only Aethelgard — vast, ancient, and divided.
The war that followed was never meant to end. It never did.

The Age of Formation

The World Takes Shape

The ten continents emerged from forces older than memory. Six races awakened. The first castles were built into the terrain itself — stone meeting slope, wall meeting world. No one knew it was already a war.

Year 0 — The Sundering

The First War

Avaloria burned. Shadowvale grew darker. The six races chose sides for the first time — and discovered that once chosen, sides are not easily abandoned. The war that would define Aethelgard forever had begun.

Year 982 A.C.

The Age of the Ashen Crown

Emberhold legions mass for a southern push. Shadowvale strengthens coastal defences. Wildlands grow unstable — raider activity increasing across three continents. The floating isles of Windspire remain inaccessible without airships. The war council meets at Riverwatch. A map is drawn. Sides are chosen again.

War Council Map — recovered from the siege of Riverwatch, Year 982 A.C.

The Eternal War Continues
